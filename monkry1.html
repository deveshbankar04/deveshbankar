Monkeys are intelligent, social, and playful primates found across Africa, Asia, Central, and South America. With over 260 species, they are divided into Old World and New World monkeys. Most monkeys live in trees, using their long limbs and tails for balance and movement. Their omnivorous diet includes fruits, nuts, leaves, insects, and sometimes small animals.

Monkeys live in social groups called troops and communicate through various sounds, gestures, and facial expressions. Their human-like characteristics make them fascinating animals and vital members of forest ecosystems.
