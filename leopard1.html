Leopard

Graceful Predator

The leopard (Panthera pardus) is a graceful and powerful big cat known for its distinctive dark spots called rosettes. Found across parts of Africa and Asia, it boasts the widest distribution of all wild cats and can thrive in a variety of habitats, from rainforests to arid mountains. Leopards are primarily solitary and nocturnal predators, using their exceptional stealth and camouflage to stalk and ambush prey.

Renowned for their strength and agility, they are excellent climbers and often haul large kills into tree branches to keep them safe from scavengers like lions and hyenas. While the species as a whole is listed as Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List due to habitat loss and human conflict, populations vary by region, with some subspecies like the Amur leopard being critically endangered.
