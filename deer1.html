Deer

Graceful Herbivore

Deer are hoofed mammals belonging to the family Cervidae. Found on all continents except Australia and Antarctica, they thrive in forests, grasslands, mountains, swamps, and tundras. Male deer typically grow antlers that shed and regrow annually, while female reindeer are the only does that also develop antlers.

Deer are herbivores, feeding on grass, leaves, twigs, fruits, and fungi. Their four-chambered stomach helps digest tough plant material. Known for their agility and sharp senses, deer can detect predators such as wolves and big cats easily due to their wide field of vision.
