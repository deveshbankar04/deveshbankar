Bull

Powerful Strength

A bull is an intact (uncastrated) adult male of the Bos taurus cattle species, primarily known for its muscular build, strength, and often aggressive temperament, especially compared to the female counterpart, a cow. These animals typically have thicker bones, a very muscular neck, and larger feet, with many breeds also possessing horns. Mature bulls generally weigh between 500 and 1,000 kg (1,100 and 2,200 lb), and their natural lifespan can be 20 to 25 years.

Bulls play a vital role in agriculture for breeding and are central to cultural practices and sports across the globe. In many cultures, they symbolize potency, fertility, and royal power, and have been featured in ancient art, mythology (such as the Sumerian Epic of Gilgamesh), and religions. In Hinduism, the bull Nandi is worshipped as the vehicle of the god Shiva. Bulls are also prominent in modern sports like bull riding and bullfighting, though these activities are a subject of considerable controversy and animal welfare concerns.

Due to their powerful nature and potentially dangerous behavior, bulls require careful handling. A common misconception is that the color red angers bulls; cattle are in fact red-green colorblind, and it is the movement that provokes them. In modern farming, a bull's temperament and physical soundness are important considerations, with artificial insemination (AI) becoming increasingly common to improve herd genetics and avoid the risks associated with handling live bulls.
